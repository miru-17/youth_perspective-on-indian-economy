Youth Perspectives on Marriage, Family Size & Economic Impact in India
Overview

This project focuses on analyzing youth perspectives on marriage, financial stability, family size, and the economic impact of smaller families in India using Data Mining and Data Visualization techniques. The analysis was performed using Python libraries such as Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, and Scikit-learn.

The project explores how factors like age group, gender, financial awareness, occupation, and area of residence influence opinions related to marriage age, raising children, ideal family size, and the future workforce. It also applies Machine Learning techniques such as K-Means Clustering, PCA (Principal Component Analysis), and t-SNE visualization to identify different social and economic mindset groups among respondents.

Objectives
Analyze youth opinions regarding marriage and family planning.
Study the importance of financial stability before marriage.
Understand perceptions about raising children in urban and rural areas.
Explore attitudes toward AI, automation, and workforce shortages.
Identify social and economic mindset clusters using Machine Learning.
